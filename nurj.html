The Northwestern Undergraduate Research Journal

The Northwestern Undergraduate Research Journal (NURJ) is an annual student-produced journal that showcases outstanding research done by Northwestern undergraduates from across all academic fields. As the only peer-and faculty- reviewed journal of undergraduate research at Northwestern University, our goal is to make research more accessible, showcase outstanding work, and to use our online publications as a means of expanding upon what is offered by our print format.

Leading the Publication of Two Volumes

VOL. 8: "SURG TELL-ALL"

To increase accessibility of research on campus and shed light on the experience of completing an independent undergraduate research project, I co-interviewed 5 researchers about their experiences and edited 4 hours of video footage on Final Cut Pro X to create a 40 minute feature.

Each video segment covers interviewee responses to questions aimed at a specific aspect of the research process, preceding a write-up highlighting this aspect of one interviewee's project experience. I recruited and trained 5 students in AP style, coordinating timelines and delegating edits to accomplish this.


VOL. 7

This edition is a collection of excellent undergraduate research conducted Summer 2021. Our team selected, reviewed, and edited 4 undergraduate theses from authors of a variety of majors. Each author has their own biography and Q&A section to give you insight behind the scenes of who they are as academic scholars and where their research interests stem from. Following these pieces are the authors' individual pieces.
