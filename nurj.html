The Northwestern Undergraduate Research Journal

The Northwestern Undergraduate Research Journal (NURJ) is an annual student-produced journal that showcases outstanding research done by Northwestern undergraduates from across all academic fields. As the only peer-and faculty- reviewed journal of undergraduate research at Northwestern University, our goal is to make research more accessible, showcase outstanding work, and to use our online publications as a means of expanding upon what is offered by our print format.

Leading the Publication of Two Volumes

VOL. 8: "SURG TELL-ALL"

Concept

Increase accessibility of research on campus by shedding light on the experience of completing an independent undergraduate research project by featuring recipients of Northwestern's Summer Undergraduate Research Grant (SURG).

Use a unique format that capitalizes on the online format and differentiates the online publication from the print publication.

Responsibilities

  • Managing every stage of publication
  • Onboarding & training new staff

Process

(1) Soliciting, selecting, planning

Prior to outreach, I drafted a one-pager describing the project in full and outlining requirements for applicants. I provided materials to our web development team for online solicitation & coordinated with our outreach team to identify key departmental targets, professors, and groups to help publicize the opportunity.

I reviewed all submissions and research theses submitted, selecting five that stood out as impressive and that complemented one another well, spanning different disciplines and conducted in various settings.

(2) Interviewing

After landing on five umbrella categories, we drafted interview questions for each. All prior NURJ publications were formal in their presentation, and we were careful in crafting humorous and surprising questions to confront interviewee expectations and set the stage for authentic descriptions of their experiences.

I co-interviewed five recipients using rented AV equipment in my living room with Managing Director of Development Kevin Bai.

(3) Mixed-format storybuilding & Writeups

Each video segment covered interviewee responses to questions aimed at a specific aspect of the research process, preceding a write-up highlighting this aspect of one interviewee's project experience. I created team storyboarding exercises to engage newly onboarded staff in collaborative brainstorming, ultimately deciding on this format, shown at right.

I recruited and trained 5 new team members in AP style, coordinating timelines and delegating edits. Creating consistency across written segments proved a major obstacle, particularly with an unprecedented publication format. I noticed that more guidance was needed after sharing interviewee Katerina Stanisavljevic's written feature as a template, so I created a feature writeup skeleton. After initial drafts & content editing on my part, three rounds of peer-editing were assigned. The final product was reviewed by myself and Associate Managing Editors Defne Deda & Alanna Liu.

(4) Video editing

Major unexpected obstacles emerged as video & audio post-production began:

  • An understaffed development team.
  • Lengthier interviews, with five hours of footage to edit.
  • An unattainable collaborative workflow without professional and expensive video production tools to allow third-party cloud transfers of massive files.
  • Poor audio and video quality despite positive results from initial testing. Inconsistent volume, white noise, areas of mono rather than stereo audio and other pain points necessitated a crash course in Final Cut Pro X software tools.

I had to strategically balance technical work & team management by remaining adaptable:

  • To oversee key narrative choices, I took on footage from three interviewees, later editing audio/video on the other two (edited by Kevin Bai) for consistency & sorting them by question response.
  • To ensure ample time for quality content creation without stalling staff, I pushed back our publication timeline by a month and and opted to present one interviewee's uncut interview to each staff member to get drafting of writeups underway. With high quality interviewee responses, we quadrupled the planned duration of video content to 40 minutes.

(5) Publishing

After coordinating with our web development team to implement the publication format, I drafted a letter from the editor to introduce the publication. Our outreach team used our materials to publicize the release.

Outcome

A 40-minute video feature divided into five clips, with written segments highlighting one area of each of the five SURG recipients' experiences.
