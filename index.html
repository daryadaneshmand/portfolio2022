Hi, I'm Darya

I'm a third-year student at Northwestern University studying Neuroscience and Journalism and pursuing the Segal Design Certificate. I take initiative where I see opportunity spaces, and take an interdisciplinary approach to problem solving.

I find that scientists are designers by definition, harnessing the power of observation to understand systems and their relationships. By researching our environments and selves, we learn how to improve them.

Thus, observing (researching) people and the spaces they inhabit sparks my creative drive. My ability to adapt and my comfort with approaching unfamiliar territory are to me my most valuable soft skills. In the workplace, I value communication, adaptability, and transparency, and bring my initiative and drive to every endeavor I take on.

Work aside, I love spending time outdoors and on the move. I do a lot of hiking at home in San Diego and am a Padi Certified diver. Indoors, you'd likely find me reading my latest find in non-fiction or philosophical fiction, drawing, playing chess, or working on a passion project. My family jokes that live music has always been a higher budgetary priority for me than groceries, and that fact I cannot refute.

I'm an aspiring...

Scientist

My interests and academic background lie in the neurological basis of:

  • substance use disorders
  • biological clocks & sleep
  • learning & memory
  • processing of sound, music, & speech

While previously shadowing and subsequently assisting with neurofeedback sessions, I had a chance to review EEG results of patients with various psychiatric conditions and traumatic brain injuries. Seeing changes in the expression of different brain waves in specific brain regions over courses of treatment (by examining archives) furthered an existing interest in both non-invasive neuromodulation and transcranial brain stimulation.

Engineer

I began working at the Segal Prototyping and Fabrication Lab at Ford Motor Company Engineering Design Center in February of 2020. Through this initial exposure and subsequent personal projects, I gained machining experience in the following:

  • laser cutter
  • center lathe & mill
  • CNC router
  • standard woodworking equipment & procedures

More recently, I've loved exploring makerspaces in the Bay Area, where I've begun 3d modeling parametric designs with Rhino & Grasshopper and getting feedback from hobbyists outside of the university bubble!

Designer

I take a human-centered approach to product design. I've gained valuable skills in research, user observation and testing, risk identification & mitigation, and iterative prototyping. I have experience presenting materials to a panel of design reviewers and co-drafting a comprehensive final product report (see Snail Tracker Project).

My experience with branding (see Mayfest Productions) has also furthered my understanding of how visual elements can be harnessed to reflect identity and values.

Artist

As a storyteller, I'm fascinated by the many ways art can tell stories. I create digital art primarily with Photoshop, Illustrator, and Procreate, and enjoy sketching with mechanical pencil. My focus within my Journalism major has primarily been on interactive media, and I'm exploring this further through Northwestern's Bay Area Immersion Program.

A few other art forms I've explored in my free time:

  • installation art
  • creative coding
  • web design (e.g. this portfolio)
