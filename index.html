Hi, I'm Darya

I'm a senior at Northwestern University pursuing degrees in Neuroscience and Journalism along with the Segal Design Certificate.

I take an interdisciplinary approach to problem-solving and thrive in dynamic environments that allow me to bridge skillsets and draw from multiple disciplines. My ability to adapt and my comfort with approaching unfamiliar territory are to me my most valuable soft skills.

I love spending time outdoors and on the move, and do a lot of hiking at home in San Diego. Indoors, you'd likely find me working on a passion project, reading my latest find in non-fiction or philosophical fiction, playing chess, or finding new music.

I'm an aspiring...

Scientist

My interests and academic background lie in the neurological basis of:

  • substance use disorders
  • biological clocks & sleep
  • learning & memory
  • processing of sound, music, & speech

While shadowing and assisting with neurofeedback sessions, I reviewed EEG results of patients with psychiatric conditions and traumatic brain injuries, and spent time in college reviewing and publishing student research.

Engineer

I began working at the Segal Prototyping and Fabrication Lab at Ford Motor Company Engineering Design Center in February of 2020. Through this and later projects, I gained machining experience in the following:

  • laser cutter
  • center lathe & mill
  • CNC router
  • standard woodworking equipment & procedures

More recently, I've loved exploring makerspaces in the Bay Area, where I've begun 3d modeling parametric designs with Rhino & Grasshopper and getting feedback from hobbyists outside of the university bubble!

Designer

I take a human-centered approach to product design. I've gained valuable skills in research, user observation and testing, risk identification & mitigation, and iterative prototyping. I also have experience presenting materials to a panel of design reviewers and co-drafting a comprehensive final product report.

My experience with branding has also furthered my understanding of how visual elements can be harnessed to reflect identity and values.

Artist

As a storyteller, I'm fascinated by the many ways art can tell stories. I create digital art primarily with Photoshop, Illustrator, and Procreate, and enjoy sketching with mechanical pencil. My focus within my Journalism major has primarily been on interactive media, which I'm exploring further through Northwestern's Bay Area Immersion Program.

A few other art forms I've explored in my free time:

  • installation art
  • creative coding
  • web design (e.g. this portfolio)
