DILLO 50 CACTUS

With the theme of Dillo 50, Northwestern's entirely student-run music festival, being "Return of the Rodeo," I felt compelled to build a giant cactus doubling as an interactive art installation. Because why not. My roommates kindly put up with the 8 foot planks of wood that were temporarily in our living room. Students were able to respond to a prompt on a piece of paper which they could place in an insert (the cactus's spines).

Skills

  • wood cutting (jigsaw)
  • modular design
  • design planning & sketching
  • horizontal band saw

CROWDPLEASER

Never have I learned more from a group of people in such a short period of time. Crowdpleaser is a nightclub popup installation -- a group of students passionate about music came together to realize a vision. I helped with lighting, which involved soldering LED strips and building wooden frames. Watching the programming process (involving Max MSP, Arduino, and HueFlash) was inspiring, and I learned a great deal from the new friends I made on the team. I also dabbled in projection mapping software Isadora.

Skills

  • soldering
  • resource management
  • projection mapping (Isadora)

Found in Translation

My grandfather loves to drink wine and translate French poetry, among other works, to Farsi. For years, he has published his translations in a local Persian magazine based in Los Angeles. However, many younger members of my family are unable to read Farsi, and as such are precluded from engaging with any of his writings. I have been working on translating these to English and printing them in hard copy book format.
