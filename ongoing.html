DILLO 50 CACTUS

Concept

Dillo Day's 2022 theme was "Return of the Rodeo," honoring the festival's Texas roots and representing "a grand celebration of the past through a comtemporary lens." I planned to pay tribute to these roots through (1) an in-theme physical art installation design and (2) an interaction component that allowed physical components to communicate a message inherent to the choice of theme.

Spines of cacti are often pain-inducing and thus avoided. By drilling holes in 260 wooden spines, I invited viewers to write recent words or incidents that had stung them. To look forward through remembrance and documentation of the past.

Process

The cactus body was made from 8'x1' cuts of beaded 3/8" plywood panels. 260 spines were cut from 1.5"x1.5"x96" mixed grain boards (Douglas Fir) using a horizontal band saw, with slots drilled in each spine at a ~45 degree angle for placement of the rolled sticky notes. Wood glue proved impractical, so spines were hot glued. The modular design allowed for transportation of the 8 foot piece and public assembly during Sean Kingston's performance.

Outcome

An interactive installation for the main stage that, to my relief, withstood the wind and rain :)

CROWDPLEASER

Concept

A grant-funded nightclub popup installation, designed entirely by a team of undergraduate students & led by Tyler Felson.

Process

I helped with lighting, which involved soldering LED strips and building wooden frames for lighting encasements. Watching the programming process (involving Max MSP, Arduino, and HueFlash) was inspiring, and I learned a great deal from collaborators on the team. I also dabbled in projection mapping software Isadora.

Outcome

A 2-night nightclub installation with a main dance floor primarily featuring 8 audio-reactive lighting frames, infinity mirrors, and a bar repurposed to house student dj's and vj's controlling lighting patterns via pads. Two additional rooms purposed for styling and lounging: the "chill room" featured projection mapping on one wall, with seating and sweet treats doubling as an art piece hanging from the ceiling. I volunteered for various day-of shifts, working with teammates in setup and maintenance of each space through the nights.
